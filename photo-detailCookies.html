3D Printed Cookie Cutters

As a way of celebrating the holiday season my mother and I always commit to some grand baking challenge. We'll spend days at a time in the kitchen making dozens of cookies, macarons, or whatever takes our fancy and will distribute the finished product to friends and coworkers. This year we decided we were going to add my rowing team to the recipient roster and thought they needed something a bit more specialized. Thus I turned, as I often do, to 3D printing. The cookies you see were cut using 3D printed cookie cutters and painstakingly decorated by hand with royal icing. The oar cookie cutter was created by user Whale50 on printables.com while the Power T was of my own design. This was a unique engineering challenge as I had to design the cookie cutter both in a way that it matched existing imagery of the Power T and that it was actually viable as a baking tool. The STLs can be found here: Oar and Power T
