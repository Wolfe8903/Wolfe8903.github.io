Highlighted Engineering Projects

In December of 2020 thanks to the guidance of Mary Loveless and The Baylor Research Program, I was able to share my research titled: Gauging the Effectiveness of Microfluidic Gradient Devices. This piece sought to analyze the viability of two different methods of additive manufacturing, Fused deopsition modeling and Stereolithography, in the production of Microfluidic devices. This was acheived through detailed microscope image analysis. In addition to collected data the piece provides options for gradient device validation in future work.

A project for the Tickle College of Engineering, I was challenged to design a device that could accurately and repeatedly launch a poker chip. The exact distance and direction needed for each lauch was randomly chosen by instructors on the test day. For this project I chose to model and 3D print my disc launcher and created my own MATLAB code that, through the use of kinematics equations and gear ratio calculations, would output the neccesary number of turner gear rotations (upper right) and pull back distance for each launch.

The Sisyphus Table began as a summer project with the goal of making a unique coffee table for my apartment. From there it developed into a major piece in my engineering portfolio as repairs, maintainance, and upgrades to the system forced me to delve further into many unfamiliar topics such as local server hosting, CoreXY CNC systems, and electromagnetism.
